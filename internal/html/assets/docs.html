How to create bundles and recover files

There is also a CLI guide.

Overview

ReMemory protects files by:

  1. Encrypting them with age
  2. Splitting the key among people you trust
  3. Giving each person a self-contained bundle for recovery

Recovery works entirely offline, in a browser. No servers, no internet, no need for this website to exist.

Tip: No single person can access your data. They must combine their pieces â€” enough to reach the threshold you set (e.g., 3 of 5).

Creating Bundles

Three steps. Everything happens in your browser â€” your files never leave your device.

Step 1: Add Friends

Add the people who will hold pieces of your recovery key. For each, provide a name and optionally contact information.

Then choose your threshold â€” how many people must come together to recover your files.

Choosing a threshold:
  • 3 people, threshold 2: The simplest setup
  • 5 people, threshold 3: A good balance
  • 7 people, threshold 4â€“5: More secure, more coordination
High enough that collusion is unlikely. Low enough that recovery works if one or two people are unavailable.

Step 2: Add Files

Drag and drop the files or folder you want to protect.

Good candidates:

  • Password manager recovery codes
  • Cryptocurrency seeds/keys
  • Important account credentials
  • Instructions for loved ones
  • Legal document locations
  • Safe combinations
Note: Avoid files that change often. This is designed for secrets you set once and leave.

Step 3: Generate Bundles

Click "Generate Bundles" to encrypt your files and create a bundle for each person.

Each bundle is around 5 MB because it includes the full recovery tool. Recovery works even if this website is gone.

Distributing to Friends

Send each person their bundle however you prefer:

  • Email: Attach the ZIP file
  • Cloud storage: Share via Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.
  • USB drive: Physical handoff
  • Encrypted messaging: Signal, WhatsApp, etc.
Tell them:
  1. Keep the bundle somewhere safe â€” cloud backup, USB drive, a drawer
  2. They cannot use it alone; they will need to coordinate with others
  3. A single piece reveals nothing, but it should still be kept private
  4. Consider printing the README.pdf and keeping it in a safe. Paper survives digital disasters. The MANIFEST.age file can be stored separately since it's just encrypted data.

Recovering Files

What Friends Receive

Each bundle contains:

README.txt Instructions, unique share, contact list
README.pdf Same content, formatted for printing. Includes a QR code for importing the share.
MANIFEST.age Your encrypted files. Only included separately when data exceeds 5 MB.
recover.html Recovery tool (~1.8 MB), runs in any browser

Each bundle is personalized â€” the friend's share is pre-loaded, and a contact list shows who else holds pieces. When the encrypted data is small enough, it's embedded too.

Path A: I Have the Bundle ZIP

The simplest path. If you have the bundle ZIP (or the files from it):

1

Extract the ZIP and open recover.html

Open it in any modern browser. Your share is already loaded.

2

Load the encrypted manifest

For small archives (â‰¤ 5 MB), this is automatic â€” the data is already embedded. Otherwise, drag MANIFEST.age from the bundle onto the page.

3

Coordinate with other friends

The tool shows a contact list with other friends' names and how to reach them. Ask them to send their README.txt or share text.

4

Add shares from other friends

For each friend's share: drag their README.txt onto the page, paste the share text, or scan a QR code from their PDF. A checkmark appears as each share is added.

5

Recovery happens automatically

Once enough shares are gathered (e.g., 3 of 5), decryption starts on its own.

Tip: If a friend sends their entire .zip bundle, drag it onto the page â€” both the share and the archive are imported at once.

Path B: I Have a Printed PDF with Words

Each printed PDF includes your share as a list of numbered words. Type them into the recovery tool â€” no camera or scanner needed.

1

Open the recovery tool

Visit the URL printed on the PDF, or open recover.html from any friend's bundle.

2

Type in your recovery words

Find the word list on your PDF and type the words into the text area. You don't need the numbers â€” just the words, separated by spaces.

3

Load the encrypted manifest

You may need the MANIFEST.age file â€” drag it onto the page or click to browse. If you don't have it, any friend can send theirs. Every bundle has the same copy.

4

Collect shares from other friends

Contact other friends and ask for their shares. They can send their README.txt, share text, read their words over the phone, or you can scan their QR code.

5

Recovery happens automatically

Once the threshold is met, decryption starts immediately.

Tip: Words are the easiest to share over the phone. If a friend can't send their share digitally, they can read the words aloud and you type them in.

If your device has a camera, scan the QR code on the PDF to import your share directly.

Scan the QR code with your phone camera â€” it opens the recovery tool with your share pre-filled. Or visit the URL on the PDF and type the short code shown below the QR code.

2

Load the encrypted manifest

You may need the MANIFEST.age file â€” drag it onto the page or click to browse. If you don't have it, any friend can send theirs. Every bundle has the same copy.

3

Collect shares from other friends

Contact other friends and ask for their shares. They can send their README.txt, share text, or you can scan their QR code.

4

Recovery happens automatically

Once the threshold is met, decryption starts immediately.

Best Practices

Choosing Friends

  • Longevity: People likely to be reachable in 5â€“10 years
  • Geographic spread: Not all in the same place
  • Technical ability: Any mix is fine â€” the tool is designed for everyone
  • Relationships: Will they cooperate with each other?
  • Trust: A single share reveals nothing, but you're trusting them with responsibility

Security Considerations

  • Don't keep all bundles together â€” that defeats the point of splitting
  • Consider printing README.pdf â€” paper survives digital disasters
  • Save project.yml if you want to regenerate bundles later

Rotation

Consider creating new bundles every few years:

  • Friends' contact info changes
  • You may want to update secrets
  • Relationships change
  • New cryptographic best practices emerge

Revoking Access

Once a share has been distributed, it cannot be revoked. This is by design â€” there is no server, no central authority.

If you need to change who holds shares:

  1. Create new bundles with a new set of friends and a fresh passphrase
  2. Send new bundles to the friends you still trust
  3. Ask every remaining friend to delete their old bundle and replace it with the new one
Important: Old shares still work with old archives. When you send a new bundle, be clear: delete the old one, keep only the new one. No version history, no "just in case."

The same applies when secrets change. New bundles mean a new key and new shares. Old shares won't open the new archive, but they still work with the old one. Make sure friends aren't holding on to old copies.

Advanced: Anonymous Mode

When holders should not know each other's identities, use anonymous mode:

  • People are labeled as "Share 1", "Share 2", etc.
  • No contact information is collected or stored
  • READMEs skip the "Other Share Holders" section
  • Bundle filenames use numbers instead of names

When to Use Anonymous Mode

This is useful when:

  • Holders should not know each other
  • You're testing quickly without entering names
  • You have another way to coordinate recovery
  • Privacy is a higher priority than ease of coordination

How to Enable

In the bundle creator, enable the Anonymous toggle in the Friends section:

  • The friend list is replaced by a share count
  • Set how many shares and the threshold
  • Bundles are named bundle-share-1.zip, bundle-share-2.zip, etc.

Recovery in Anonymous Mode

Recovery works the same way, but without the contact list. Holders see generic labels like "Share 1" instead of names.

Important: Without a built-in contact list, make sure holders know how to reach each other when recovery is needed.

Advanced: Multilingual Bundles

Each person can receive their bundle in their preferred language. Five languages are supported: English, Spanish, German, French, and Slovenian.

How It Works

  • Each friend entry has a Bundle language dropdown
  • "Default" uses the current UI language
  • Override per person to mix languages
  • recover.html opens in the selected language
  • Anyone can switch languages at any time
